<p><p>The systemic herbicide market is divided into two primary types: selective and non-selective herbicides. Selective herbicides target specific plant species, effectively controlling unwanted weeds without harming desired crops. This makes them ideal for agricultural applications where crops and weeds coexist. In contrast, non-selective herbicides kill or damage all plant types they come into contact with, making them suitable for clearing areas of unwanted vegetation, such as driveways or industrial sites. Both types are crucial for effective weed management.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>CVD Diamonds</li><li>HTHP Diamonds</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The lab-grown diamonds market comprises two primary types: CVD (Chemical Vapor Deposition) and HPHT (High-Pressure High-Temperature) diamonds. CVD diamonds are created by synthesizing carbon gases that crystallize into diamond form, offering a variety of colors and purity levels. In contrast, HPHT diamonds mimic natural diamond formation by applying extreme heat and pressure to carbon. Both types are more sustainable and cost-effective alternatives to mined diamonds, gaining traction in the jewelry industry and among eco-conscious consumers.</p></p>
